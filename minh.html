Shoyu Ramen

What is ramen?

Ramen has taken the western world by storm, but what is this dish and what has led to it being so popular all across the world? Simply put, ramen is a Japanese noodle soup, with a combination of a rich flavoured broth, one of a variety of types of noodle and a selection of meats or vegetables, often topped with a boiled egg. In Japan ramen is considered a fast food, with many small restaurants and street vendors offering a warming bowl of this delicious soup.

Ramen

10% noodles, 90% love.

  • Prep: 15 mins
  • Cook: 10 mins
  • Total: 25 mins
  • Servings: 2 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 teaspoon minced fresh ginger
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1 cup store-bought or homemade kombu dashi soup stock
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sake
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2 (3-ounce) packages dried chukamen noodles
  • Negi, or spring onion, chopped, for optional garnish
  • Nori, dried seaweed, for optional garnish
  • Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

A Note from Our Recipe Tester

"This broth is very satisfying. A quick hack is to use plain water and a piece of kombu in place of the kombu stock; remove the kombu when it comes to a boil. Customize this any way you like, adding pork or chicken or mushrooms for a filling and comforting meal." —Rick Horiike

Method

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Heat sesame oil in a deep pan over medium heat. Sauté the chopped ginger and garlic in the pan for about a minute.
  3. Lower the heat and add the chicken soup stock and kombu dashi soup stock to the pan. Bring to a boil.
  4. Add the soy sauce, sake, sugar, and salt to the soup and bring to a boil again.
  5. In the meantime, boil water in a large pot. Add the chukamen noodles to the boiling water and cook for a few minutes (follow package directions).
  6. Place a fine-mesh strainer over a bowl and pour the soup through the strainer.
  7. Pour the hot soup into individual bowls.
  8. Drain the noodles and add to the hot soup.
  9. Add toppings, such as chopped negi and nori seaweed, if desired. Sprinkle with pepper to taste.
  10. Enjoy!
